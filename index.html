"Carbo" the Carbon Atom

Hi, I'm Carbo, a carbon atom.  At the moment I'm a part of a carbon dioxide molecule (CO2) in the atmosphere.  Carbon dioxide makes up less than 0.05% of the atmospheric gases.  Some of the other major gases are nitrogen (N2), oxygen (O2), water vapor (H2O), and hydrogen (H2).  Carbon atoms are absolutely essential to life on earth.  They are the basic building blocks for organic molecules such as proteins, carbohydrates, fats, and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).

I always get so warm, up here in the atmosphere.  It must be because I'm a greenhouse gas.  Along with certain other atmospheric gases, such as water vapor, ozone (O3), and others, I absorb a portion of the heat radiating upwards from the surface of the earth, and reflect it back down.  The glass panes in a greenhouse function in a way similar to these atmospheric gases, and keep the inside very warm.  Greenhouse gases help the earth retain heat that would otherwise be lost into space, and thus maintain atmospheric temperatures higher than they otherwise would be.  The activity of these gasses is often referred to as the greenhouse effect.

I've heard that carbon atoms cycle through the earth and its biosphere.  I think I would like to explore this process myself.
